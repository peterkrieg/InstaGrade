About

MediaScore is an Instagram analytics and grading tool. It displays your media and info in unique ways, and provides a score to your account. It was created by Peter Krieg as a project for the coding bootcamp Dev Mountain. I had a ton of fun making this and have memorized my Instagram photos in the process. It is an attempt to show employers that I can code and that I would like a job please!

FAQ

How Does MediaScore Work?

When you sign in with Instagram, the web app gets lots of data from Instagram—your media, people who have liked your media, the media you have liked. The program processes this information and bundles it into a "report" which represents a snapshot of your profile at that instant. By comparing multiple reports over time, the program can give extensive info about your account.

What Does MediaScore do With My Data?

We just show it back to you, and it remains completely private. Instagram requires all apps to follow strict privacy policies in order to access user data. See the privacy section for more details.

How do I Generate Another Report For My Account?

Go to your account section by clicking the user icon in the top right, and account on the drop down menu. Click the "new report" button.

Why Are the Reports So Slow to Load?

To gather all of your data, the program needs to make hundreds, usually thousands of requests to Instagram. While this is a bit faster with a good internet connection, the limiting factor is the speed Instagram sends the data back, which is out of the program's control. As long as you don't close your tab and come back later, the data should be loaded eventually. The report loading can take up to 45 minutes for large accounts.

How Does MediaScore Know Who Has Unfollowed Me?

By comparing consecutive reports, the program can see who has unfollowed you—and give the time range of when that happened by comparing the times of the reports.

Why Am I Limited to One Report Per Day?

To save space on storage—reports can take up to 10MB in size for larger accounts! (currently working on reducing size). But maybe there will be future premium paid account for reports every hour, eh?

What do the Scores Mean?

They are a percentage, supposed to be like a school grade. So, 90-100 is an A, 80-89 a B, 70-79 a C, etc.
